# Interpretation Notes for UCPIS

**Status:** Informative / Non-Normative  

---

## Purpose

This document lists common misinterpretations of UCPIS and clarifies the
intended reading.

---

## Common Misinterpretations

### “UCPIS is a standard”
Incorrect. UCPIS defines no compliance requirements and asserts no authority.

### “UCPIS can be implemented directly”
Incorrect. UCPIS is not an implementation guide.

### “UCPIS governs humans or AI”
Incorrect. UCPIS constrains interfaces, not actors.

### “Deferred annexes are unfinished”
Incorrect. Deferral is intentional and evidence-gated.

### “Interoperability profiles are coming next”
Incorrect. Profiles require empirical evidence and may never be produced.

---

## Correct Framing

UCPIS should be understood as:
- a thinking tool,
- a shared language,
- and a long-horizon architectural reference.
